# PRD: Адвокатско дружество "Гунчева и Карагачлиев"

## Product Summary

A clean, modern, trustworthy static website for a two-partner law firm based in Plovdiv. The site should make potential clients feel confident reaching out — fast to load, easy to navigate, and reassuringly professional without being corporate or overwhelming.

## Product Vision

Establish a calm, credible digital presence that reflects the firm's values: professional competence, personal attention, and 10+ years of practice since 2016. Visitors should immediately understand who the lawyers are, what they handle, and how to get in touch.

## Problem

Potential clients searching for legal representation in Plovdiv find either no online presence or overly complex law firm sites that feel impersonal. The firm needs a simple, mobile-friendly site that converts visitors into consultations.

## Goals

- Present the firm's identity and practice areas clearly in under 5 seconds
- Make contact information immediately accessible (phone, address, map)
- Build trust through clean design and lawyer profiles
- Be fully static — zero maintenance burden
- Evoke competence, warmth, care, dedication, experience, and trustworthiness through every design and content choice

## Trust & Emotion Requirements

Research shows 46% of credibility is judged from visual design alone, before a word is read (Stanford Web Credibility Project). Visitors decide within 50ms whether a site feels trustworthy. Every element either builds trust or erodes it. For a law firm — where clients share sensitive personal and legal problems — this is critical.

### How trust is built (by design element)

| Feeling | How to achieve it |
|---------|------------------|
| **Competence** | Clean layout, generous whitespace, no clutter. Consistent typography, aligned grids, flawless spacing. A visually disciplined site signals a disciplined lawyer. |
| **Warmth** | Plovdiv imagery (local, real, human). Warm accent tones (terracotta, warm stone). Copy that speaks plainly, not in legalese. |
| **Care** | Lawyer bios with detail — bar numbers, years, specific experience. Transparency about process ("how we work"). Phone number always visible — never hidden behind a form. |
| **Dedication** | Full practice area coverage — 8 areas with clear descriptions. Shows breadth of expertise. Specificity signals commitment. |
| **Experience** | The 1880 law book image (legal tradition). Years in practice (est. 2016) prominently stated. Bar registration numbers in bios. |
| **Trustworthiness** | Physical address with map (you exist). Real phone, real email. No stock photos, no gavels, no scales of justice. Authentic Plovdiv imagery. Fast load time (under 2s) — slow sites signal incompetence. |

### Visual trust signals (specific)

- **Color**: White background + near-black text + one warm muted accent. Avoids aggressive reds, neons, or cold clinical blues. Warm neutrals feel approachable (research: Germans prefer blue, but a Plovdiv firm should take cues from local terracotta and stone).
- **Typography**: One or two typeface families max. 16px+ body text, generous line-height (1.6+). Readability over trendiness.
- **Layout**: Low visual complexity — the homepage should feel calm, not busy. Research shows low-to-moderate visual complexity creates favorable first impressions and higher trust (Tuch et al., 2009).
- **Imagery**: Real Plovdiv (tova.jpg, Mrkvička painting, law book). No stock photography of any kind. Authenticity beats polish.
- **Whitespace**: Generous. Content that breathes signals confidence. Cluttered layouts signal anxiety or amateurism.
- **Mobile**: Most legal searches happen on phones, often in stress. If mobile is cramped or slow, trust is lost before desktop is ever seen.
- **Speed**: Under 2s load time on mobile data. Every second of delay reduces trust measurably.
- **No errors**: Zero typos, zero broken links, zero placeholder text. A spelling mistake on a law firm site reads as negligence.

### Content trust signals (specific)

- **Bios**: Full names, bar registration numbers, years of practice, education, specific experience areas. Generic bios = generic lawyers.
- **Contact**: Physical address + map + phone + email. All visible in footer on every page. Phone in header on every page. A firm that hides its contact info is a firm with something to hide.
- **Practice areas**: Specific descriptions, not just labels. "Административно право" → a short paragraph explaining what this means for the client. Show you understand their problem.
- **Transparency**: Consider a short "Как работим" section explaining the process — first consultation, documentation, communication. Reduces anxiety for first-time legal clients.
- **Testimonials (future)**: Named testimonials with specific outcomes. Vague anonymous praise does nothing. BrightLocal research shows reviews from named sources are trusted significantly more.

### Concrete choices
- White background, black/dark gray text
- Plovdiv hero photo with dark overlay on Начало
- Abstract hill-inspired forms (layered curves) as section dividers on other pages
- Generous whitespace, readable type (16px+ body)
- No carousels, no sliders, no animations
- Lawyer bios use typographic hierarchy + small abstract accent
- Map and contact block in page footer on every page
- Mobile-first — most legal searches happen on phones

### This avoids
- Heavy tiered menus or mega-menus
- Cluttered sidebars or widget blocks
- Dense walls of legalese text
- Lawyer portraits (by preference)
- Auto-playing media or parallax effects
- Stock photography of gavels, scales of justice, or other legal clichés

## Functional Requirements

- Responsive (mobile, tablet, desktop)
- Phone number tappable on mobile (tel: link)
- Embedded Google Map on Contacts
- Static HTML/CSS/JS — no build step, no backend
- Semantic HTML for accessibility
- Fast load (under 2s)

## MVP Scope

- 4 pages: Начало, За нас, Услуги, Контакти
- Embedded map + click-to-call
- Lawyer bios with abstract form accents (no photos)
- No blog, no testimonials (add later if desired)

## Non-Goals (v0)

- No blog or news section
- No multi-language
- No analytics or tracking
