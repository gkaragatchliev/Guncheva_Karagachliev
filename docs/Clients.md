# Client Persona — Visitor's Perspective

## Persona

A Bulgarian adult (25–65) seeking legal help — could be an individual with a property dispute, a small business owner needing contract review, or someone facing criminal charges. They are stressed, time-sensitive, and judging the firm within seconds of landing on the page.

## Mindset

- **Sceptical** — "Can I trust these people with my problem?"
- **Impatient** — "Do they do what I need? How do I reach them?"
- **Visual-first** — judges competence by design quality before reading a word

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. First Impression (< 3 seconds)
- Does the hero image feel relevant to Plovdiv / Bulgaria?
- Is the headline clear about what the firm does?
- Do I immediately know this is a law firm?

### 2. Trust & Competency
- Do the bios sound like real people I could talk to?
- Does the firm have a clear origin story (2016, Kapana)?
- Are practice areas listed in plain language?
- Does the site feel modern or outdated?

### 3. Findability
- Can I find the phone number immediately?
- Is the address clearly written?
- Can I see working hours without hunting?
- Is there a map I can open on my phone?

### 4. Mobile Experience
- Does everything fit my phone screen?
- Can I tap the phone number to call?
- Is the menu easy to open/close on mobile?

### 5. Language & Tone
- Is everything in Bulgarian?
- Does it use simple words (not legalese)?
- Does it feel like it's written for me, not for other lawyers?

## Verdict

> **Desirability**: Would a client bookmark this site or bounce?
> **Competency**: Does the design project competence or amateurism?
> **Conversion**: Does the visitor pick up the phone or leave?

## Recommendation Framework

For any score below 4, recommend:
1. **What's wrong** — the specific barrier
2. **Why it matters** — the client's emotional state
3. **Fix suggestion** — what to change
